Laureano Gonzalez

About

PhD candidate whose research is focused on monitoring sedimentation in water resources using fibre optic temperature sensing. Extensive international experience in the oil and gas industry as a senior reservoir engineer and facilitator of reservoir studies aiming to optimise petroleum recovery and projects’ profits. Motivated by the principle of a more sustainable society, I decided to undertake the PhD program to expand my academic, professional and general skills, and apply them into projects aiming to incorporate practices and technologies to optimise natural resources and mitigate climate change.



Skills

Research skills

  • Developed a methodology to monitoring sedimentation and storage capacity changes using fibre optic distributed temperature sensors coupled with heat pulses.
  • Used creative problem-solving and solution methods in an academic project during the Innovation Management course, where I proposed a diversified business model involving saltwater waste from the oil and gas industry, aquaculture activities and Queensland communities.
  • Identified factors affecting the production of petroleum fields such as adverse mobility ratio, and designed/modelled enhanced production methods to improve recovery.

Interpersonal skills

  • Represent and advocate for the research student community on the USC’s Research Degree Committee.
  • Coordinated short and long-term activities and studies for reservoir teams in the oil and gas industry.

Technical skills

  • Proficient user of Microsoft Office (OneNote, Excel, PowerPoint, Word).
